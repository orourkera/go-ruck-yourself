Do Weighted Vests Help Bone Density?

Short answer: there’s promising evidence in older adults and during weight loss that vests help maintain—or sometimes improve—BMD. For younger, healthy folks, it’s a smart long-game habit alongside protein and strength training.

What the studies say

  • Long-term hip preservation: Women using weighted vests in an exercise program prevented expected hip bone loss over a year.
  • During weight loss: Daily vest use in older adults undergoing diet-induced weight loss helped preserve hip/spine BMD vs controls.
  • Comparisons: Resistance training remains powerful; vest + walk is a practical additive.

Practical protocol

  • 2–4 sessions/week of vest walking; start at 5–10% BW.
  • Layer in 2–3 strength sessions (squats/hinges/steps/pushes/pulls).
  • Protein 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day; vitamin D and calcium per guidelines.

Sources

  • Long-term weighted-vest exercise prevents hip bone loss (2000).
  • Weighted vest use during dietary weight loss preserves BMD (2018).
  • Weighted Vest or Resistance Exercise to Offset Weight-Loss Bone Loss (JAMA Net Open).
