How Many Calories Does Rucking Burn?

Rucking increases energy expenditure over normal walking by adding external load. Calorie burn depends on bodyweight, pack weight, pace, terrain, and duration. A light-to-moderate ruck can rival easy running for total calories if performed longer.

Key Variables

  • Bodyweight: Heavier individuals expend more energy at the same pace.
  • Ruck weight: More load increases work—progress gradually.
  • Pace and grade: Faster walking and hills significantly boost burn.
  • Surface and terrain: Trails and sand increase demand versus pavement.

Simple Estimation Method

As a rough guide, add 10–20% to brisk-walking calories for each meaningful load increase (e.g., +10–20 lb), adjusting for hills and pace. Our app refines this with your distance, time, elevation gain, and optional heart rate.

Improve Calorie Burn Safely

  • Extend duration before increasing weight.
  • Add gentle hills or intervals (e.g., 3–5 min brisk, 2–3 min easy).
  • Use good posture and shorter steps to maintain efficiency.
  • Track heart rate to stay in an aerobic zone for longer sessions.

Common Mistakes

  • Jumping weight too quickly—leads to hotspots and overuse issues.
  • Leaning forward excessively—strain on low back and neck.
  • Ignoring footwear and socks—blisters reduce consistency.
